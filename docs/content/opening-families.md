# Opening Family Selection Rationale (v1)

Per `DEVELOPMENT_PLAN.md` §2 GAP (Open Question #8), the system defines 6 core opening families for instructional purposes (§12.4), but only 3 are targeted for the v1 application to minimize content-authoring bottlenecks.

The following three families were selected for v1:

1. **Open centre (Italian Game)**
   * **Instructional purpose:** Rapid development, central tension, king safety.
   * **Rationale:** Essential foundational knowledge for beginners. The Italian Game provides clear, direct tactical themes and emphasizes early development and castling.
2. **Positional queen-pawn (Queen's Gambit)**
   * **Instructional purpose:** Pawn tension, space, minority structures, development.
   * **Rationale:** Contrasts with the open center by introducing slower, positional plans and the importance of pawn tension over immediate clashes.
3. **Black against 1.e4 (Caro-Kann Defense)**
   * **Instructional purpose:** Central response styles and defensive planning.
   * **Rationale:** Provides the user with a reliable, solid repertoire as Black. The Caro-Kann emphasizes strong pawn structures and patient defensive planning, complementing the aggressive white repertoire choices.

## Excluded Families (Deferred to post-v1)
* Tactical gambit (King's Gambit) - deferred as it represents a more high-risk, specialized learning path.
* Closed or flank (English Opening) - deferred in favor of prioritizing the core d4 and e4 plans.
* Black against 1.d4 (Indian systems) - deferred to limit initial scope; users will face 1.d4, but formal guided instruction is slated for a future release.
